\section{Introduction}

Neutral hydrogen emits radiation at a wavelength of 21 cm, and corresponds to a frequency of 1420MHz. 
This emission of hydrogen is commonly used in radio astronomy to study the structures of galaxies. 
The data used in this project is data collected from UCA's radio telescope, and the main purpose is to analyze the data to identify potential hydrogen emissions.

The data that the radio telescope came from Casseiopeia A (CasA), which is a well-known supernova remnent that has been extensibely studied in radio astronomy \cite{chandra_casa}.
Cassieopeia A is also one of the brightest extrasolar radio source in the sky, making it a good object to collect data from.

Another source of data used is from Virgo A, a supergiant elliptical galaxy located about 53.5 million light years away from Earth \cite{plotner_virgoA}.

As stated above, data collected from both of these sources were used to try and detect potential hydrogen emmission.
